Cairo, Egypt

Cairo is the capital city of Egypt. It is one of the largest cities in Africa. For many years, Cairo has stood on the banks of Nile. It has served as a gateway to the Nile delta which is located in the northeast of the country. This separates into the Rosetta and Damietta branches.

Cairo's people was one ethnically and religious diverse. Now, Cairo is a moslty muslim country. A number of Egyptian Christians live in various areas of the city, majority of which observe Coptic Orthodox faith. In other areas, old Italian, Greek, Syrian, and Sudanese comminuties are still found. Status differences in the population of Egypt on a person's birth of origin or their modernity degree. Around half of Cairo's population are in the city proper. The other half of the population are in the suburbs.

For a long time in the Middle East, Cairo has been a cultural capital and the chief of mass media centre in the region. Cairo has also been the area for the region's religious and cultural institutions. In the 19th century, the introduction of different European cultutal insitutions took place. Amongst these include theatres such as the Baroque Opera House, found on the Opera Square in downtown Cairo. This was destroyed by fire in 1971 and was later on replaced with a newer structure in 1988.

Lagos, Nigeria

Lagos city is a city and chief port of the Lagos state of Nigeria. It served as the capital of Lagos state until 1975. In December 1991, Lagos city became the federal capital of Nigeria. This has been replaced now. Ikeja became the state capital, and Abuja became the federal capital. However, in many government agencies, Lagoes remained as the unofficial seat.

In Lagos Island is where Lagos' population is found. This is situated on Lagos Lagoon, on the Bight of Benin in the Guld of Guinea. Lagos is known to be Nigeria's largest city. Lagos is also one of the largest sub-Saharan Africa.

In the 15th century, Yoruba fishermen and hunters settled in Lagos Island. They are called Oko. From the late 16th century to mid-19th century, Lagos Island has been taken over by the Kingdown of Benin. They are called Eko.

In 1472, Portuguese people came to Lagos Island. Trade developed around this time. This was halted a century later when the Portuguese were granted asientos de negros-monopolies. This was to sell slaves in the Spanish Americas. Kings (also known as local obas), liked the good relationship between them and the Portuguese. They establoshed a growing slave trade. In 1851, the British attempts to stop the trade. They sent out a naval attack on Lagos and overthew the oba. It was not until 1861 when Lagos was under british control when the slave trade stopped.

Johannesburg, South Africa

Johannesburg is a city in the Gauteng province of South Africa. Johannesburg is the country's chief industrial and financial capital. In 1886 was when Johannesburg was first founded. This was followed by the discovery of gold. Initially, Johannesburg was a part of the Transvaal republic. Transvaal is an independent Afrikaner, or Boer, which became part of the four provinces of South Africa. Now, Johannesburg is part of the Gauteng. Gauteng is one of the nine provinces of South Africa.

Johannesburg's division is reflected from a century of racially driven social engineering, racial segragation in South Africa between 1948 to 1994. This resulted in a city of contrast. That is, area of abundance and richness on one side, and an area of poverty on the other.

Johannesburg has a rich cultural life. You have abundance in arts. Johannesburg is has a sunny climate. People spend a lot of time outdoors. In the northen part of the city, there is a broad space for bird-watching and picniking. People here are also avid sportsmen. After the return from political isolation, Johannesburg hosted different international competitions. Among these include rugby, football, cricket, golf, and tennis.
