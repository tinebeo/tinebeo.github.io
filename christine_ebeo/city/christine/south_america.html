Lima, Peru

Lima is the city and capital of Peru. Lima is considered as Peru's commercial and industrial centre. Lima's nickname is El Pulpo (“The Octopus”). Lima is the a fourth of the total population of Peru. This is a result of concentration of people, capital, political influence, and social innovations. For a long time, Lima was the only point of contact between the country and other countries.

Lima's city layout is strongly influenced by its long history. The checked street layout comes from Spanish colonists during the 16th century. Lima also has many colonial buildings. For example, The Presidential Palacen and many more reflect the past popularity of the French Empire style.

Lima's population has transformed since the 1930s. It is now hard to find a true Limeño. Everywhere, one can hear different tones, accents, which reflects different origins who have come to this city. Before arrival of migrants, it was easy to tell between European and other ethnic mixtures. Modern-day Lima, however, classification is no longer easy. Currently, the only classification that persists is rich and influencial versus poor and powerless.

In spite problems that face Lima and those those who reside in the city, it is still the dominant and most vibrant cultural centre of Peru.

Montevideo, Uruguay

Montevideo city is the principal city and capital of Uruguay. It found on the north shore of the Río de la Plata estuary.

In 1726, Montevideo was founded by Bruno Mauricio de Zabala. During this time, Montevideo was mostly a Spanish town. During its early years, Montevideo was mostly a Spanish garrison town. As trade expanded, Uruguay gained more independece.

During the early 1800s, Montevideo was occupied by British, Spanish, Argentine, Portuguese, and Brazilian forces. During this time, trade and population declined. This lasted for years, but it was not until the Montevideo succeeded the in 1851 when Montevideo became the major port of the Río de la Plata, gaining the power back.

Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

Rio de Janeiro is the city and port capital Brazil. Rio de Janeiro is located on the Atlantic Ocean. This is city is known to be amongst the world's most beautiful and most interesting urban centres.

Rio de Janeiro's name was given by Portuguese navigators. They arrived to the city in 1502 and mistakenly thought the entrace of the bay as the month of a river. In Portuguese, rio is the word for river.

Rio de Janeiro is known for its beautiful beaches, peaks of ridges, and hills covered by tropical forests. Rio de Janeiro is a centre of leasure not just for Brazilian people, but also foreign tourists as well.

Rio de Janeiro is an economic centre. The activity ranges from inductry, trade, administration, banking, education, culture, and research.
